RNGPIT Landing Page Redesign

A Fresh Gateway to Academic Excellence

Overview

The RNGPIT Landing Page Redesign was executed as a rapid, high-intensity 1-week design sprint focused exclusively on reimagining the institution's primary digital front door. Academic portals frequently suffer from information bloat, burying critical conversion points under dense layers of administrative text.

My singular task was to take a massive volume of disconnected institutional requirements and synthesize them into a single, high-converting, visually striking landing page. The objective was to balance the needs of prospective students looking for admissions with the credibility markers required by corporate recruiters and accrediting bodies.

Structural Implementation Strategy

To transform the institutional blueprint into a streamlined digital experience, the final layout organizes the college’s offerings into 10 distinct sections. Rather than relying on complex navigation, the interface follows a clear scroll-based hierarchy.

The journey begins with a strong Hero Section featuring key impact metrics, followed by About Us and the Message from the Principal/Chairman to establish credibility and institutional identity. Once trust is built, the page shifts to its academic focus through the Academics (Programmes) grid.

The lower half emphasizes validation and engagement through Our Recruiters, Why Choose Us?, and Accreditation & Recognition highlights, including achievements such as NAAC A+ status and placement milestones.

The experience concludes with community-focused content, including an Admissions Spotlight, Past Events & Announcements, Upcoming Events, and a multi-column Footer that supports quick access to important information.

Figma Workspace Review Note: For detailed insights into the UX strategy, typography hierarchy, and layout decisions developed during this one-week sprint, please refer to the annotations and comments placed throughout the master frame.

Sprint Reflections & Takeaways

Executing a complete institutional layout transformation in just seven days proved that high information density does not have to create visual clutter. By replacing traditional academic complexity with a conversion-focused narrative scroll, the sprint delivered a cleaner and more engaging user experience.

The final design successfully balanced the needs of prospective students, corporate recruiters, and institutional stakeholders within a single cohesive framework. This project demonstrates how agile design thinking can transform complex requirements into an intuitive, scannable, and modern digital experience under a tight deadline.
